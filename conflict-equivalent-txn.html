Conflict Equivalent Transactions and Conflict Serializability

Introduction

Conflict equivalent transactions and conflict serializability are concepts used in database concurrency control to ensure that transactions are executed in a manner that maintains data consistency.

Conflict Equivalent Transactions

Two schedules are conflict equivalent if they involve the same operations and the order of conflicting operations is the same. Conflicting operations are those that access the same data item and at least one of them is a write operation.

Conflict Serializability

A schedule is conflict serializable if it can be transformed into a serial schedule by swapping non-conflicting operations without changing the result. A serial schedule is one where transactions are executed one after the other without interleaving.

Example

Schedule:
T1: R(x) W(x)
T2: R(x) W(x) R(x)
                    
Conflict Serializable:
Yes, if T2 operations can be reordered without affecting the final state.
